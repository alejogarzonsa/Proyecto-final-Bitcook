Boyacense-stew

It is one of the most famous and consumed typical Boyacá dishes in the department. This is one of the examples of recipes from Spain that arrived with the colony. Over time it adapted to the foods that were most produced in the region. The boyacense stew has Creole chicken, sabanera potatoes, ibias, bacon and a variety of other ingredients that give it a unique flavor.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 90 minutes.
  • Preparation: 30 minutes.
  • Cooking: 60 minutes.

Instructions

  1. Over medium heat, place a frying pan with 2 tablespoons of oil, let it heat for a minute, add the onion and sauté for a minute.
  2. Add the tomato and cilantro, the water, the pork rib, the beef, the maggi rib broth envelope, the cob, the broad beans, the peas, the cubios, the chuguas and the baby sabanera potato.
  3. Cook for 20 minutes or until all ingredients are soft; Finally, add the baby Creole potato and cook for 5 more minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and serve.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons of oil (28 ml)
  • 2 stalks of long onion, finely chopped (30 g)
  • 2 tomatoes without skin, finely chopped (246 g)
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped cilantro (10 g)
  • 3 cups of water (1000 ml)
  • ½ pound pork ribs, cut into 4 pieces (250 g)
  • ½ pound beef cut into 4 pieces (250 g)
  • 1 sachet of shredded maggi rib broth (9 g)
  • 1 ear cut into 4 pieces (400 g)
  • ¼ pound fava beans (125 g)
  • ¼ pound shelled peas (125 g)
  • ½ pound of cubes (125 g)
  • ½ pound of chuguas (125 g)
  • ½ pound of baby sabanera potatoes with skin (250 g)
  • ½ pound of baby Creole potatoes (250 g)
