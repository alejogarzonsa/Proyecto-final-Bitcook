Backbone-cuchuco

Cuchuco is a preparation made with corn or wheat, it is consumed regularly in the Cundiboyacense highlands. Its texture allows the soups to be thick, which added to a variety of tubers, peas, onion and cabbage, results in a great food. This preparation is very pleasant, but, without a doubt, adding the pork backbone will make this one of the most desired dishes in Boyacá.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 90 minutes.
  • Preparation: 30 minutes.
  • Cooking: 60 minutes.

Instructions

  1. Submerge the cuchuco in water for 15 minutes, after time, drain, rinse about 3 times until the water comes out clean and set aside.
  2. Place a pot where you normally prepare soups over medium heat with the oil until it is hot, add the onion and sauté until it is transparent.
  3. Add the cilantro branch, the pork backbone and sauté for 3 more minutes, add the two varieties of potatoes, the beans, the carrot, the peas, the maggi rib broth and stir for 2 more minutes.
  4. Add the cuchuco to the water and let it cook for 25 to 30 minutes or until the soup thickens and all the ingredients are soft. Don't forget to remove the cilantro branch.
  5. Remove from heat, serve and sprinkle with chopped cilantro.

Ingredients

  • ¼ pound of wheat cuchuco (125 g)
  • 1 tablespoon oil (14 g)
  • 1 stalk of long onion finely chopped, including the green part (15 g)
  • 1 sprig of cilantro (10 g)
  • 1 pound pork backbone cut into 4 equal parts (500 g)
  • ¼ pound Creole potato cut into cubes (250 g)
  • ¼ pound pastusa potatoes cut into cubes (125 g)
  • ¼ pound fava beans (125 g)
  • 1 small carrot cut into small cubes (50 g)
  • ¼ pound fresh green peas (125 g)
  • 1 sachet of maggi rib broth (9 g)
  • 4 cups of water (1000 ml)
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped coriander leaves (20 g)
